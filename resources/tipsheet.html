  7. Test Tips from Student Comment (New! As of 7/15/2019)
    • Kaiser Criterion. If you have attended my Sunday Live Webinars you may have heard me talk about it. The Kaiser Criterion states that PC below and eigenvalue of 1.0 can be dropped (ignored).
    • Homoscedasticity. It means the variables have essentially the same variance. We see this in linear regression where the data points all follow the regression line closely. Its opposite is heteroscedasticity which means that there is a greater dispersion of the data points relative to the regression line.
    • T-tests. It appears that t-tests are back on the exam and you are required to calculate a t-value of a set data from two groups. Our new slides this month will deal with t-tests. So, look for them. Meanwhile, practice your t-tests given the means, variance, and n of each group.
    • Read the questions carefully. Take your time. Read each question and the responses at least twice. Then, perform a process-of-elimination to find the Best Answer.
    • Calculator. Several students have reported that the test is much less calculator dependent as in prior versions.
    • Eigenvalues. There have been no eigenvalue calculation problems recently. So, you will not have to solve the quadratic equation and factor your terms as in the past.
    • F-statistic. Be sure to watch and practice F-statistic calculations and solving the ANOVA table given missing terms.
