Name One Sample T-Test or Student's T-Test
Definition Tests the mean of a randomly selected sample taken from a normally distributed population when the population standard deviation is unknown.
Hypothese \( H_0 : \mu = \mu_0 \)
\( H_a : \mu > \mu_0 \)
Input Values Group Mean, Standard Deviation, Number of data points or raw data
Output Values t-score, p-value
Interpretation of Output A large t-score indicates a larger difference between the groups. The p-value indicates the probability that the result is caused by chance. /td>
Value of the Method Useful when comparing two normally distributed groups when the population standard deviation is unknown.
